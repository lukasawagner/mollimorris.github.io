Molli Morris

Molli Morris (b. March 17, 1999, Colorado, USA) is an American director's assistant and MFA candidate at Maine Media. After beginning her career in fashion, she transitioned into film, combining both disciplines in a distinctively rockstar style.

She lives in Los Angeles, California, and enjoys leathercrafting, wood carving, figure drawing, poetry, cooking, and beach lounging.

Early Life

1990s: Born in Centennial, Colorado to parents Jeff and Tina, Molli was raised with two brothers. Her family background includes farmers, cinder block entrepreneurs, and park rangers.

2000s: Moved to Boston, took art classes, and entered fashion at 13. Noticed by the CFDA and inspired by Petra Collins, she dreamt of Marfa, Texas.

2010s/2020s: In high school, Molli was student body president and art-focused. Attended St. Lawrence University, painting and working at Tory Burch. Also attended RISD summers.

Career

Molli started at Tory Burch before COVID-19 led her to Marfa. She later moved to L.A., working with stylists like Nancy Steiner. She joined R&D Placement in 2022 and has supported Prettybird, Strike Anywhere, and Group Thrpy. In 2024, she became a Director's Assistant.

She also co-directs the documentary series Life At Night and practices leathercraft to reconnect with fashion. She's pursuing her MFA at Maine Media.
